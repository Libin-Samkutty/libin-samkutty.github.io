Most teams automate the expected path and stop there. Failures happen outside expected paths - in edge cases, async flows, and AI responses that drift from their training distribution. QA that only verifies happy paths gives false confidence. The work is exposing unmodeled behavior before it reaches users.

  • Automate what is repeatable. Reserve judgment for what cannot be scripted. Writing Botium flows for AI responses made this concrete: automation verified routing; only a human could judge whether the response was actually appropriate.
  • Observability belongs inside quality engineering, not downstream of it.
  • A test suite that never fails isn't comprehensive - it's incomplete. The Nivi health pipeline caught silent production failures that infrastructure monitoring missed entirely.
  • AI systems need evaluation infrastructure, not just manual spot-checks.
