AI systems can't be tested the way regular software can. There's no pass/fail assertion for "did the LLM give a harmful answer" or "did the RAG pipeline retrieve the right context." You need evaluation infrastructure that can measure relevancy, faithfulness, context precision and context recall across a wide range of metrics, golden datasets, trace instrumentation, and continuous monitoring, not just a test suite.

  • Traditional QA catches regressions. AI quality engineering catches drifts when model behavior shifts without a code change, when retrieved context degrades silently, when a prompt that worked last month stops working now.
  • Observability belongs inside quality engineering, not downstream of it. The Nivi health pipeline caught silent production failures that infrastructure monitoring missed entirely.
  • Evaluation without ground truth is still evaluation. Relevancy, faithfulness, and context precision are measurable metrics, you just need the right framework and the discipline to track them over time.
